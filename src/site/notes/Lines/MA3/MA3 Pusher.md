The MA3 Pusher is a two-part mechanical assembly at the end of the [[Lines/MA3/MA3\|MA3]] line. After cartridges are advanced along the line by the rake, the pusher advances pairs of two [[Definitions/Maxwell RSC\|Maxwell RSC]] cartridges simultaneously into alignment zones on the [[Unknown/Drop Gate\|Drop Gate]], where they are loaded into trays. Once all trays (approximately 8, unverified) are loaded, they are suctioned up and stacked at the end of the line. The accuracy of the pusher's lateral position is critical — any offset translates directly into misalignment at the gate. Active redesign work is tracked in [[My Projects/MA3 Pusher Redesign\|MA3 Pusher Redesign]].

#Definitions

## Assembly

The pusher is a stainless steel assembly consisting of two main parts joined by two screws.

The fork is a 3-4 mm thick stainless steel plate with a slight stepped profile, suggesting it was fully machined from 7-8 mm stock rather than formed from sheet metal. On the underside, there is a recessed rectangle that fits around the pneumatic base it mounts to, creating a lip that holds it in place. A pin on the base mates with a matching hole in the fork to lock its rotational position. A bolt hole on the base mates with a slotted hole on the fork, allowing slight adjustment for tolerance. The fork extends out and splits into two prongs, each with a countersunk hole to accept the mounting screws.

The pusher plate is a small rectangular piece with holes that match the countersunk holes on the fork. The two parts are fastened flush using flat head screws. The face of the plate that contacts the cartridges is flat, with two vertical slots that align with a small plastic tab on the [[Definitions/Maxwell RSC\|Maxwell RSC]] cartridges. The top of the plate has a slope to it, and the bottom is flush with the fork along the push plane.

The original screws are believed to have been ordered by [[Definitions/Optima\|Optima]], the vendor who manufactured MA3. The closest known equivalent is a 4-40 stainless steel flat head screw (McMaster-Carr 91781A106).
