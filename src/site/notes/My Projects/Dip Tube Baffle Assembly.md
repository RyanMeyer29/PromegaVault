#My-Projects

The Dip Tube Baffle Assembly project is a design and fabrication project done for [[People/Philip McKeefry\|Philip]]. He is collaborating with the [[Definitions/In Vitro Dispensing\|IVD]] team on this project. The assembly is a 2L sinker shroud with two metal barbed hose connectors that needed redesign. The opposite side of the barb piece includes some kind of bubble protection feature (bubble catcher or releaser — exact function unclear).

The barb pickup pieces needed to be redesigned to better fit two different hose sizes — one small and one large. The pieces also needed to be mirrored/centered, as the original part was off-center. The reference drawing for the original part is SAP #1015703, a 316L stainless steel 2L sinker shroud with 2 barb welds.

## Design History

Produced two prototype barb pickup pieces based on the original drawing and a small plastic barb reference part:
- 4.8 mm tubing barb piece — sized based on an existing barb used elsewhere at Promega for 4.8 mm tubing
- 3.2 mm tubing barb piece — scaled from the 4.8 mm version (2/3 size)
- Both pieces included same-size bubble protection, with the 4.8 mm version scaled to 3/2 of the 3.2 mm side
- Total width target: <= 25 mm
- Printed 3 different sizes on the [[Fabrication/Formlabs Form 3L\|Formlabs Form 3L]]

After a second meeting, redesigned to produce 3 more larger versions (slightly smaller than prototype 1, using intuition to determine sizing). Philip then met with the [[Definitions/In Vitro Dispensing\|IVD]] team separately to select preferred sizes. They chose to move forward with the largest of both size options.
