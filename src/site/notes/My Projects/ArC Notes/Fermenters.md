#Definitions

Fermenters are the sealed, sterilized vessels used in [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Fermentation\|Fermentation]] to grow bacteria under controlled conditions. At Promega, fermenters come in three sizes. The 30-liter vessels are P4 and P5. The 300-liter vessels are P1 and P2. The 3000-liter vessels are P3 and P6.

Each fermenter has an [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Agitator\|agitator]] with a mechanical seal (a key sterilization point), addition lines for adding [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Fermentation Media\|media]] or feed components during a run, an exhaust line that connects back to a shared exhaust tank, and harvest lines that connect to the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Centrifuge\|centrifuges]]. All of these lines and surfaces must be sterilized via [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Sterilize in Place\|SIP]] before use.

Fermenters are controlled through the [[Software/Batch\|Batch]] recipe system. In multi-vessel recipes, smaller fermenters are used to grow initial [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Biomass\|biomass]], which is then transferred into a larger vessel for the main fermentation and eventual harvest. Some [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Fermentation Media\|media]] components cannot be sterilized together (they caramelize), so they are sterilized in separate vessels and transferred in together.
