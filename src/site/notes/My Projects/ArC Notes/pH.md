#Definitions

pH is a measure of how acidic or basic a solution is, on a scale from 0 to 14. A pH of 7 is neutral (pure water), below 7 is acidic, and above 7 is basic (alkaline). In [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Fermentation\|Fermentation]], pH is one of the key variables monitored and controlled. Every organism has an optimal pH range for growth, and straying too far outside that range can slow or stop growth entirely. Before [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Inoculation\|Inoculation]], the fermenter is adjusted to the target pH. It is then monitored and controlled throughout the run.
