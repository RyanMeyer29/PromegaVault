#Definitions

Biomass refers to the total mass of living organisms growing in a fermenter at any given point — in Promega's context, this usually means bacteria. The biomass phase is the period after [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Inoculation\|Inoculation]] where the organism is allowed to grow and multiply. Operators monitor growth over time and take samples to track progress. The goal is to reach a sufficient quantity of organism before either harvesting directly or proceeding to [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Induction\|Induction]] to trigger protein expression.

In multi-vessel recipes, biomass is grown in smaller vessels first and then transferred into a larger vessel to continue growing before harvest.
