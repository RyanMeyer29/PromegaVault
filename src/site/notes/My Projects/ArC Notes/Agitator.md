#Definitions

An agitator is the mechanical mixing component inside a fermenter vessel. It spins to stir the culture, which serves two main purposes: distributing [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Dissolved Oxygen\|Dissolved Oxygen]] evenly throughout the liquid, and keeping the bacteria and nutrients suspended rather than settling. The agitator is driven by a motor and passes through the vessel wall via a mechanical seal — this seal is a critical sterile boundary point and is the first thing sterilized via [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Sterilize in Place\|SIP]] before each run, because it is the most vulnerable path for contamination to enter.

Agitator speed (RPM) is one of the parameters set in the [[Software/Batch\|Batch]] recipe for each fermentation phase.
