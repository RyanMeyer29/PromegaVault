#Definitions

Dissolved oxygen (DO) refers to the amount of oxygen present in solution in the fermenter culture. Most bacteria used in fermentation require oxygen to grow, so maintaining adequate dissolved oxygen is critical during the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Biomass\|Biomass]] phase. [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Agitator\|Agitation]] helps distribute oxygen evenly throughout the vessel. Operators can choose to actively control dissolved oxygen to a setpoint (using agitation speed, airflow, or both) or simply supply air and let DO float — the choice depends on the organism and product being grown.
