#Definitions

Supernatant is the liquid fraction that remains after centrifugation during fermentation harvest. When the culture is spun in the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Centrifuge\|centrifuges]], the cells pellet out as [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Cell Paste\|Cell Paste]] and the liquid above them is the supernatant. For products that are secreted outside the cell (rather than expressed inside), the supernatant is the valuable fraction. At Promega, most products are intracellular, so supernatant is less commonly the primary product — but it is still collected and may be processed depending on the product.
