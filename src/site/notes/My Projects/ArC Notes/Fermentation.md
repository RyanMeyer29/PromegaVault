#Definitions

Fermentation is a biological manufacturing process used to grow a specific organism — typically bacteria — under tightly controlled conditions. The goal is to produce large quantities of a target organism or the protein it expresses, without contamination from any other organism. At Promega, fermentation takes place in the [[Buildings/Arnold\|Arnold Center]] and is used to produce raw material ([[My Projects/ArC Notes/Cell Paste\|cell paste]] or [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Supernatant\|supernatant]]) that feeds downstream purification and product manufacturing processes.

Key variables monitored and controlled during fermentation include [[My Projects/ArC Notes/pH\|pH]], [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Dissolved Oxygen\|dissolved oxygen]], [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Agitator\|agitation]] speed, temperature, and the composition of [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Fermentation Media\|media]] and feed components. The organism is grown in sealed, sterilized vessels called [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Fermenters\|Fermenters]]. After reaching the target [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Biomass\|biomass]], the culture is either harvested directly or first goes through [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Induction\|Induction]] to express a target protein, then transferred to [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Centrifuge\|centrifuges]] for harvest.

Products can be expressed inside the cell (most common at Promega) or outside the cell in the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Supernatant\|supernatant]]. After harvest, [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Cell Paste\|cell paste]] or supernatant is frozen on-site or shipped to downstream facilities like [[Buildings/RFC\|RFC]] for further processing. The fermentation process is controlled and sequenced using a recipe system called [[Software/Batch\|Batch]], operated through [[Definitions/HMI\|HMIs]] and [[EWS\|EWS]] workstations.
