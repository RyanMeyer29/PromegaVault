#Definitions

Induction is a step in [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Fermentation\|Fermentation]] where conditions inside the fermenter are deliberately changed to trigger the bacteria to express (produce) a specific target protein. It happens after the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Biomass\|Biomass]] phase, once there is enough organism in the tank. The idea is to first grow a large population of bacteria under conditions optimized for growth, then switch to conditions that make those bacteria produce the desired product. Not all fermentation runs require an induction step — it depends on the product.

At Promega, induction is one of the defined phases in the [[Software/Batch\|Batch]] recipe system and happens before harvest lines are sterilized and the transfer out to the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Centrifuge\|centrifuges]] begins.
