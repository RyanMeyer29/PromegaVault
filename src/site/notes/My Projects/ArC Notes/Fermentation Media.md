#Definitions

Media (fermentation media) is the nutrient solution that bacteria grow in inside the fermenter. It contains everything the organism needs to survive and multiply — carbon sources, nitrogen sources, salts, vitamins, and other components depending on the organism and product. Media is loaded into the fermenter vessel before sterilization and is sterilized together with the vessel at Promega (some facilities sterilize them separately, but Promega sterilizes both together).

In some recipes, additional feed components are added to the fermenter during the run through addition lines — these also need to have their lines sterilized via [[Definitions/Steam in Place\|SIP]] before use. Certain media components cannot be sterilized together because they will caramelize at high temperatures; in multi-vessel recipes, those components are sterilized in separate vessels and combined afterward via transfer.
