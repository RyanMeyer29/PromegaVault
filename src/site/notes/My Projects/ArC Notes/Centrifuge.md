#Definitions

Centrifuges are used in the fermentation harvest process to separate [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Cell Paste\|cell paste]] from the culture liquid ([[My Projects/ArC Notes/Supernatant\|supernatant]]) after growth is complete. At Promega there are 8 centrifuges on the harvest line. They spin at approximately 17,000 RPM and are described as very large — roughly as tall as a bus. Culture is pushed into them from the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Fermenters\|fermenter]] using air pressure, and pumps at the centrifuges control the flow rate. Flow rate determines separation efficiency.

Harvest lines running from the fermenters to the centrifuges must be sterilized via [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Sterilize in Place\|SIP]] before use. After centrifugation, the resulting [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Cell Paste\|cell paste]] or [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Supernatant\|supernatant]] is either frozen on-site or shipped to a downstream facility like [[Buildings/RFC\|RFC]] for further processing.
