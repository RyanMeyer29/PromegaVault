#Definitions

Sterilize in Place (SIP) is a sterilization method used to sterilize fermentation vessels and associated lines without disassembling them. The general concept is that sterilization is performed in place — the medium used to sterilize can vary, but at Promega it is done with steam, bringing the vessel or line up to 121°C and holding that temperature for at least 15–30 minutes before cooling back down in a controlled manner.

At Promega's fermentation area in the [[Buildings/Arnold\|Arnold Center]], SIP is performed in a specific sequence for each vessel: the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Agitator\|agitator]] seal is sterilized first (it is the most vulnerable sterile boundary), followed by the exhaust line, and then the full vessel. Every addition line and path going into the vessel must also be sterilized as part of the vessel SIP. The exhaust system connects back to a single shared tank, which adds complexity to exhaust SIP. All of this is managed using manual hand valves, which makes timing and safety important.

SIP is one of the defined phases in the [[Software/Batch\|Batch]] fermentation recipe system. It also applies to harvest lines (sterilized before transfer out to the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Centrifuge\|centrifuges]]) and to transfer lines between [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Fermenters\|Fermenters]] in multi-vessel recipes.
