#Definitions

Inoculation is the step in [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Fermentation\|Fermentation]] where the target organism (typically bacteria) is introduced into the sterilized fermenter vessel in a sterile way. Before inoculation, the vessel has been sterilized via [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Sterilize in Place\|SIP]], cooled to growth conditions, and adjusted to the correct [[My Projects/ArC Notes/pH\|pH]]. Once inoculated, the organism begins growing and the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Biomass\|Biomass]] phase starts. Maintaining sterility during inoculation is critical — any contamination introduced at this step is very difficult to trace and eliminate.
