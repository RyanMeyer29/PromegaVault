#Definitions

Cell paste is the solid fraction recovered after centrifugation during fermentation harvest. When the culture is spun in the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Centrifuge\|centrifuges]], the bacteria (cells) are separated from the liquid they were growing in. The cells pellet out into a thick paste. Most of Promega's fermentation products are expressed inside the cells, so the cell paste is the valuable material — it gets frozen on-site (sometimes for up to a year) or shipped to a downstream facility like [[Buildings/RFC\|RFC]] for further processing such as protein purification.

The liquid fraction left over after centrifugation is called the [[My Projects/ArC Notes/Supernatant\|Supernatant]].
