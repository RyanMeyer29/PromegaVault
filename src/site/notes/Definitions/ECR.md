#Definitions

An Environmentally Controlled Room (ECR) is a specially designed space where environmental parameters such as temperature, humidity, particulate contamination, and air pressure are precisely regulated. ECRs at Promega are governed by ISO 14644, which outlines requirements for air exchange rates, pressure differentials, particulate monitoring, and HEPA filtration. The Facilities department is responsible for maintaining, alarming, and coordinating testing for ECR areas. The terms ECR and [[Definitions/Cleanroom\|Cleanroom]] are used interchangeably at Promega — there is no meaningful distinction between them in practice.
