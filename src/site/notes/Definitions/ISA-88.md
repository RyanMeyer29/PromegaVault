#Definitions

ISA-88 (also called S88) is an international standard for batch process control. It defines a common framework and terminology for how batch manufacturing systems should be structured — including the hierarchy of control layers from individual devices up through full recipes. The standard is widely used in pharmaceutical, biotech, and food manufacturing industries.

At Promega, the [[Software/Batch\|Batch]] fermentation control system follows the ISA-88 model. The layered hierarchy of Control Modules, Equipment Modules, Phases, Operating Procedures, and Unit Procedures all come from this standard.
