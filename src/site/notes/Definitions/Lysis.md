#Definitions

Lysis (verb: lyse) refers to the process of breaking open a cell by rupturing its membrane to release its internal contents — DNA, proteins, enzymes, or other molecules of interest. It is typically one of the first steps in any molecular biology workflow, since most assays and techniques require access to material that is otherwise locked inside the cell.

Cells can be lysed in several ways depending on the application: chemical detergents, heat, mechanical force, or specialized lysis buffers formulated for specific cell types. For example, before running a [[Definitions/Polymerase Chain Reaction\|PCR]], the DNA must first be extracted from cells — lysis is how that extraction begins. Without it, the [[Definitions/Polymerase\|polymerase]] has nothing to work with.
