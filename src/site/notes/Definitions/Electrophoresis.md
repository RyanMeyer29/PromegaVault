#Definitions

Electrophoresis is a laboratory technique used to separate molecules — typically DNA, RNA, or proteins — based on their size and electrical charge. A sample is placed in a medium and an electrical current is applied, causing the molecules to migrate through the medium. Smaller molecules move faster than larger ones, so they separate out over time. The result is a pattern that can be read to identify or analyze the molecules present. At Promega, electrophoresis comes up in both product development and product use — the company makes products used in electrophoresis workflows, and uses electrophoresis techniques internally.

## Gel Electrophoresis

Gel electrophoresis is the most common form of electrophoresis. Samples are loaded into wells cut into a slab of gel (typically agarose for DNA, or polyacrylamide for proteins) and an electric current is applied. The molecules migrate through the gel matrix at different rates depending on their size, producing distinct bands that can be visualized under UV light or with a stain. At Promega, gel electrophoresis trays are used to run gels — these are physical trays that hold the gel and provide the wells for loading samples. See [[My Projects/Gel Electrophoresis Tray\|Gel Electrophoresis Tray]].

## Capillary Electrophoresis

Capillary electrophoresis (CE) performs the same basic separation but inside a narrow capillary tube filled with a polymer solution rather than a flat gel slab. It offers higher resolution, faster run times, and automated detection compared to gel electrophoresis. The [[Definitions/Spectrum\|Spectrum]] device is a Promega capillary electrophoresis instrument used to analyze DNA — most commonly for forensics and paternity testing applications. [[Definitions/Septa Mats\|Septa Mats]] are used with the Spectrum device to seal the plate during a run.
