#Definitions

A polymerase is an enzyme that builds new chains of nucleic acids — either DNA or RNA — by linking together individual nucleotide building blocks. The name comes from "polymer" (a long repeating chain) combined with "-ase," the standard suffix for enzymes.

There are two main types encountered in molecular biology. DNA polymerase copies DNA by reading an existing template strand and synthesizing a new complementary strand alongside it. RNA polymerase transcribes DNA into RNA, which is the first step of gene expression. In the context of [[Definitions/Polymerase Chain Reaction\|PCR]], it is DNA polymerase doing the work — specifically a heat-stable variant called Taq polymerase, which survives the high temperatures required during the denaturation step. Taq comes from a bacterium called Thermus aquaticus that naturally lives in hot springs.
