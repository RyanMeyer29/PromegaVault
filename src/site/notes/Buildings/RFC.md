#Buildings

The Rosalind Franklin Center (RFC) is a Promega building located at 2800 Woods Hollow Road, out east off the Beltline in Madison, WI. Named after Rosalind Franklin, the chemist and X-ray crystallographer whose work was instrumental in discovering the structure of DNA. Based on SharePoint documents, RFC is associated with large-scale protein purification. Cell paste produced in the fermentation building is shipped there, sometimes after being frozen on-site for up to a year. Roughly 3–4 fermentation batches feed into 1 batch at RFC. I likely won't visit RFC during my co-op.
